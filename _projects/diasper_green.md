DiASper Green was a Danish-German educational project focused on digital technologies, environmental measurement, climate protection, and STEM education. The project aimed to give young people practical experience with tools used to collect and analyse environmental and climate data.

My role was to design and develop three hands-on learning concepts where pupils used micro:bit-based sensor systems to measure the environment:

- [Air quality measurement](https://www.teknologiundervisning.dk/elev-opgaver/diasper-luftkvalitet/)
- [Rain gauge](https://www.teknologiundervisning.dk/elev-opgaver/diasper-regnmaaler/)
- [Water quality measurement](https://www.teknologiundervisning.dk/elev-opgaver/diasper-vandkvalitet/)

For each concept, I created the physical 3D models in Fusion 360, developed the electronic circuits, programmed the micro:bit logic using block-based MakeCode, and produced the pupil-facing guides.

The learning activities introduced pupils to sensors, circuits, programming, and data collection:

- The air quality concept measured temperature, humidity, CO₂, and air quality
- The rain gauge used a tipping mechanism with a Hall sensor and magnet to detect rainfall
- The water quality concept combined sensors such as temperature, soil moisture, and light

I was also responsible for organising and hosting a DiASper Green workshop at Økolariet in Vejle, where around 100 pupils aged 14–16 from Denmark and Germany participated. The workshop focused on environmental measurement, micro:bit programming, data collection, and basic data processing.

![Økolariet - workshop location]({{ '/assets/resources/diasper-green-oekolariet.png' | relative_url }})

As part of the workshop, I created learning materials that introduced pupils to measuring with micro:bit and working with collected data on a computer:

- [Data processing on computer](https://www.teknologiundervisning.dk/elev-opgaver/diasper-databehandling-computer/)

The data processing guide teaches pupils how to access collected micro:bit data, view it in a browser, export it, and continue working with it in Excel.

## Responsibilities

- Designed three environmental sensing learning concepts
- Created 3D models in Fusion 360 for the physical builds
- Developed electronic circuits for micro:bit-based sensor setups
- Programmed micro:bit systems using MakeCode
- Created educational guides and teaching materials
- Organised and hosted an international workshop (~100 students)
- Supported pupils in measuring and analysing environmental data
