STEPS is a continuation of earlier cross-border educational initiatives such as DiASper Green, focusing on equipping students with competencies in digital technologies, sustainability, and future-oriented skills.

In this project, I am responsible for developing learning concepts within **robotics and automation**, aimed at lower secondary education.

## Project focus

The goal is to introduce pupils to industrial robotics through accessible, simulation-based learning. The concepts are designed to bridge theory and practice, giving students insight into how automation is used in real-world production and engineering contexts.

A central tool in the project is **Universal Robots UR Studio**, a professional robot simulation environment that allows students to program and test robot behaviour in a virtual setting.

- [About the STEPS project](https://steps-projekt.eu/dk/om-projektet)
- [UR Studio](https://www.universal-robots.com/products/ur-studio/)

## My role

- Designing educational concepts focused on robotics and automation
- Developing structured student assignments using UR Studio
- Translating complex industrial robotics concepts into accessible learning activities
- Creating teaching materials and exercises for classroom use

## Pilot testing

A first test of the learning materials has been conducted with a group of **10 ninth-grade students**.

During the test:
- Students worked with the UR Studio simulator
- They completed the assignments I designed
- The focus was on understanding robot movement, programming logic, and automation workflows

The pilot provided valuable insights into:
- How quickly students engage with simulation-based robotics
- Which concepts require additional scaffolding
- How to balance complexity and accessibility in teaching industrial tools

## Perspective

The project builds on experience from DiASper Green, expanding from environmental sensing to **automation and robotics**, and continues the focus on hands-on, practice-oriented STEM education.

The long-term aim is to give students early exposure to technologies used in industry, helping them better understand future education and career pathways within engineering, production, and digital technologies.
