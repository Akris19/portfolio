A playful mobile app prototype designed to help people understand when their plants need water. Through simple visuals, animated feedback, and plant customisation.

## The idea

Din Planteven started as a larger IoT concept where sensors could measure soil moisture and send plant health data to the user’s phone. During the project, the scope was reduced to focus on the mobile app experience and create a functional prototype.

The goal was to design a simple app that gives users a quick overview of their plants’ health and helps them build a better watering routine. The app uses a sunflower character, a watering can, and visual health stages to communicate whether the plants need attention.

## Target

The app was designed for people who like having plants but do not know much about plant care — especially users who forget to water their plants, overwater them, or do not want to spend too much time researching plant needs.

The focus was on creating a fast and friendly user journey where the user could understand the status of their plants with as few clicks as possible.

## The process

The project began with inspiration from existing apps such as Plant Nanny and Copenhagen App. I then created low-fidelity sketches to explore the structure, navigation, and core features before building the prototype in Unity. All visual assets were created in Adobe Illustrator and exported as PNG files.

The prototype included a home screen where the sunflower and watering can changed depending on the overall plant health. The user could also add and personalise plants by choosing plant type, pot colour, name, and sensor connection. Animations were added to make the sunflower and background feel more alive.

Testing was conducted with three users. They tested the prototype individually while I observed, took notes, and asked follow-up questions. The test showed that the navigation bar and button placement worked well, and the animations gave life to the app without being distracting. However, the calendar icon was misunderstood, the visual style lacked depth, and the plant customisation flow needed clearer guidance.

The main learning from the project was the importance of planning, early user testing, and building iteratively instead of spending too much time polishing details before the core experience has been validated.
